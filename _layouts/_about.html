Heya, I'm Zinzy, and I’ve been yelling at Internet clouds since 1997.

You've reached my home on the Internet. In my 90s Spice Girls-themed bedroom, I taught myself how to build websites. I haven’t been the same since. At some point during this journey, making things for the Internet became my profession.

By now, I have sixteen years of professional experience researching, designing, and building digital products. I'm currently wrapping up my time at edtech startup Leeruniek. Before that I spent five years leading design in travel technology at Airtrade. Between 2007 and 2015 I ran Studio Zinzy.

I'm a UX researcher and designer specialized in making tools for people at work. I'm currently not available for contracting or full-time roles.

I come from a long line of reluctantly-Catholic, burgundian band geeks.

In the Deep Dutch South, I learned to embrace the health benefits of making music, the forests of the German border, and the importance of charcuterie.

These days, I live in Amsterdam's finest neighborhood, de Indische Buurt. There, I share a shoebox and a petit garden with my partner Anja and our dog Lemonade (who may or may not have a blog, let's find out together).

Living here and with them, I'm learning about the necessity of belonging, the beauty of quietude, and the importance of a good vacuum cleaner.
